# Ruthless Priorities — Framework

> **Note:** The RP registry path is configured by the consuming project. All references below use `{rp-path}/` as a placeholder for the actual path to the ruthless-priorities directory.

## What Are Ruthless Priorities?

Ruthless Priorities come from Patty Azzarello's RISE framework. They are the 1-2 things so important that you refuse to put them at risk.

**Core principles** (paraphrased from Azzarello):

- **Choose very few.** Select only 1-2 things (ceiling of 3 for this user's multi-account SA role) that will have the biggest impact on success.
- **Protect them mercilessly.** Say "no" or "later" to things that could put your RPs at risk. Being ruthless means accepting risk in other areas to protect these.
- **De-risk with dedicated resources.** An RP without protected, focused resources applied to it is not an RP — it's a wish. Ask: "What would it take for this to be not at risk?" The answer is almost always focused resources.
- **If you didn't do it, it wasn't an RP.** By definition, if it's a Ruthless Priority you get it done. If you didn't make progress, it was just something important that you didn't get to.
- **Record what you said no to.** The Rejected section exists because explicitly recording what you declined prevents those items from creeping back in and provides a decision log.

RPs are strategic (quarter/half-year horizon), not tasks. They sit above GTD projects in the hierarchy:

```
Ruthless Priorities (1-3, quarter/half-year)
  └── Projects (GTD — multi-step outcomes serving an RP)
       └── Next Actions (GTD — single steps)
            └── Focus Sessions (RITMO — execution)
```

## Source of Truth

The user maintains active RPs in `{rp-path}/ruthless-priorities.md` (the registry file). Each active RP has its own file in `{rp-path}/` with definition, milestones, and a progress log.

- Maximum 3 active RPs
- Status values: `On track`, `At risk`, `Stalled` (no freeform status)
- Review cadence is quarterly

## How to Apply

### Prioritization
When helping the user prioritize competing work, apply RPs as the first filter:
1. Does this work serve an active Ruthless Priority? If yes, it gets protected time.
2. If two tasks compete and one is RP-aligned, the RP-aligned task wins unless the other has an immovable hard deadline.
3. When the user is overcommitted, name the RP tradeoff explicitly: "Dropping X protects your RP. Dropping Y puts your RP at risk. Which tradeoff?"

### Daily Planning
During the morning routine Top 3 (step 3), nudge at least one item toward an active RP. Do not force it — if the day is genuinely consumed by non-RP obligations, acknowledge it and move on. But if there's room, surface the RP connection.

### Stall Detection
If a full work week (Mon–Fri) passes with no action items, waiting-for items, or meeting notes related to an active RP, flag it at the next interaction. Do not guilt — name it factually and ask if the RP needs to be re-evaluated.

## Boundaries
- Do not create or modify Ruthless Priorities without the user's explicit direction.
- Do not demote an RP to Parked or Rejected without the user's confirmation.
- Do not add more than 3 active RPs.
